Medical Disclaimer

Not Medical Advice

The content provided on this website, including but not limited to text, graphics, images, guided meditations, breathing exercises, and other material, is for informational and educational purposes only. It is not intended to be a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

The information on this platform represents general wellness practices and should not be construed as specific medical advice for your particular situation.

Always Seek Professional Advice

Never disregard professional medical advice or delay in seeking it because of something you have read on this website. If you think you may have a medical emergency, call your doctor or emergency services immediately.

Reliance on any information provided by this website is solely at your own risk. We encourage you to consult with qualified healthcare providers regarding any questions or concerns you may have about your mental or physical health.

Mental Health Support

The mindfulness practices, meditation techniques, and wellness exercises described on this site are complementary wellness tools. They are not treatments for clinical mental health conditions such as depression, anxiety disorders, PTSD, bipolar disorder, or other psychiatric diagnoses.

While mindfulness has been shown to have various benefits (as cited in our research references), it should not replace professional mental health treatment when needed.

If you are experiencing thoughts of self-harm or suicide:

  • Emergency Services: Call 911 (US) or your local emergency number immediately
  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: Call or text 988 (US) - Available 24/7
  • Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741 (US) - Free, 24/7 support
  • International Association for Suicide Prevention: Find crisis centers worldwide

No Guarantee of Results

Individual results from mindfulness practice may vary significantly. The benefits cited on this site are based on general research studies and population averages, and may not reflect your personal experience.

Factors such as individual physiology, consistency of practice, underlying health conditions, and personal circumstances all influence outcomes. We make no guarantees regarding specific results.

Evidence-Based Claims

Where we cite research studies regarding the benefits of mindfulness (such as stress reduction, improved focus, or cortisol level changes), these references are to peer-reviewed studies available in scientific literature. However:

  • Research results represent group averages, not individual outcomes
  • Study conditions may differ from self-guided practice
  • New research may update or contradict previous findings
  • We strive to keep citations current but cannot guarantee real-time updates

Privacy & Data

Any self-assessment tools or journaling features on this platform store data locally on your device unless explicitly stated otherwise. We do not have access to your personal reflections or assessment responses. For more information, please review our Privacy Policy.
